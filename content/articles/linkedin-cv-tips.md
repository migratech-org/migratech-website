## LinkedIn:

- Add a Generalised version of CV to the featured section in LinkedIn
- Add Skills
    - Skills - Top skills(10), Normal Skill(50)
    - In the main Profile section
    - In each Job Description
- Write Job Title/Role in the Profile Headline section below name
- Create a Banner image to showcase more about your profile
- Get Recommendations & Endorsements from colleagues preferably seniors
- Make the Profile summary to be more descriptive
- Write the Job Description for each role in more detail in LinkedIn compared to the resume
- Add pinned/Featured post on your profile
- Participate actively on LinkedIn as it is good for LinkedIn Algorithm
    - Always reply to recruiters
    - Create Posts, comments and like on others post
    - Connect and reach-out to the people in the positions/industries you are seeking opportunities/managers/HR
- Assign yourself a unique username in LinkedIn

Resume/CV:
- Remove "About me" and “personal summaries” sections from the resume
- Resource: Course for Writing Resume: [https://www.coursera.org/specializations/english-interview-resume](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/english-interview-resume)
- Write your achievements along with results/numbers/improvements/achievements (eyecatching) and do not just paraphrase the "Job description"
- Personalise the Resume as per the Job Description for each application
- Choose a readable font size (not too small to save space) and contrasting colours like black/use bold or italic with white background
- Share or apply with PDF format only
- Link the company page and its location in the CV especially if it's a big company
- Shorten the CV and make it clearer with the required Buzz/action words
    - 1 Pg resume is preferable
    - 2-4 bullets for each Role
    - Avoid cluttering
- Move the job experience section to the begin of the CV
- Avoid appearing as a job hopper in your CV
- If you are targeting senior role, showcase additional skills like social/people/leadership skills
- Have a Xing profile, if you are targeting Germany
- Reply to even rejections emails seeking Feedback as there is no harm in reaching out
- Remove photo from CV to avoid any potential stereotyping
- Having too much experience when compared to the Job Description may over-qualify you as per ATS
- Use standardised language levels (Eg. A1...B2)
- Adding extracurricular activities related to your experience are . Like adding Github projects, medium
- Using Latex template: [https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/deedy-cv/bjryvfsjdyxz](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/deedy-cv/bjryvfsjdyxz)
- If you have many skills, separate them into two sections “Top Skills” and “Normal Skills”
- Send the CV to the right people (English companies for example)
